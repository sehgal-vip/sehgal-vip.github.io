Hello World: Why I Started This Blog

This is where the compartmentalized chaos begins.

I’ve been meaning to start writing for years. Not the professional kind—the kind of reports and documentation that fills most of our days—but the exploratory kind. The kind where you start with a half-formed thought and see where it leads.

The Intersection of Ideas

What fascinates me most is where different domains collide:

  • AI and Philosophy: What does it mean for machines to “think”? How do large language models challenge our notions of understanding and consciousness?
  • Product Management and Human Behavior: Every product decision is ultimately about understanding people—their needs, fears, and aspirations.
  • Technology and Culture: How do the tools we build shape the societies we live in?

What to Expect

This blog will be a mix of:

  1. Deep dives into AI concepts, explained through first principles
  2. Philosophical musings on technology and its implications
  3. Product thinking frameworks and lessons learned
  4. Book notes and synthesis of interesting ideas

I don’t promise consistency or polish. But I do promise authenticity—these are the questions I genuinely grapple with.

Why “Compartmentalized Chaos”?

The name reflects how I think. My mind is full of seemingly unrelated threads—philosophy, code, psychology, design. Individually, they might seem chaotic. But when organized into compartments, patterns emerge. Connections form. That’s what this blog is about: finding order in the chaos.

Let’s see where this goes.

If you want to follow along, subscribe to my Substack, The Curiocity Loop, where I explore these ideas in more depth.
